Pet Care Guidelines

Everything you need to know about keeping your pet healthy and happy

üçñ Nutrition & Diet

Puppies (2-12 months): High-protein puppy formula 3-4 times daily. Transition to adult food gradually around 12 months.

Adults (1-7 years): Quality dry kibble or wet food twice daily. Portion size depends on weight and activity level.

Seniors (7+ years): Senior formula with joint support, lower calories. May need softer food for dental health.

Foods to Avoid: Chocolate, grapes, onions, garlic, xylitol, avocado, and cooked bones.

Kittens (2-12 months): High-protein kitten formula 3-4 times daily. Essential for growth and development.

Adults (1-10 years): Quality cat food twice daily. Cats are obligate carnivores and need meat-based protein.

Seniors (10+ years): Senior formula with kidney support. Monitor weight and adjust portions accordingly.

Foods to Avoid: Onions, garlic, chocolate, dairy products, raw fish, and bones.

Hydration: Always provide fresh water. Consider a water fountain to encourage drinking.

  • Feed at consistent times each day
  • Use measuring cups for portion control
  • Limit treats to 10% of daily calories
  • Transition food gradually over 7-10 days
  • Keep food and water bowls clean
  • Monitor weight regularly
  • Consult your vet for specific dietary needs

üíâ Essential Vaccinations

Core Vaccines (Required):

  • DHPP: Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza - Start at 6-8 weeks, boosters every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks
  • Rabies: Given at 12-16 weeks, then every 1-3 years depending on local laws

Non-Core Vaccines (Based on Lifestyle):

  • Bordetella: For dogs in boarding, daycare, or dog parks
  • Leptospirosis: For dogs exposed to wildlife or standing water
  • Lyme Disease: For dogs in tick-prone areas

Core Vaccines (Required):

  • FVRCP: Feline Viral Rhinotracheitis, Calicivirus, Panleukopenia - Start at 6-8 weeks, boosters every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks
  • Rabies: Given at 12-16 weeks, then every 1-3 years

Non-Core Vaccines (Based on Lifestyle):

  • FeLV: Feline Leukemia for outdoor cats or multi-cat households
  • FIV: Feline Immunodeficiency Virus for high-risk cats

Important Reminders:

  • Keep vaccination records up to date
  • Schedule annual wellness exams
  • Discuss vaccine protocol with your vet
  • Watch for reactions after vaccination (rare)
  • Some vaccines require boosters every 1-3 years
  • Puppies/kittens need a series of shots, not just one
  • Pregnant pets should not be vaccinated

Always consult with your veterinarian to create a personalized vaccination plan for your pet.
